How Threats Disturb Wildlife

Wildlife faces numerous threats that disturb their habitat, survival, and reproduction. Habitat destruction due to deforestation, urban sprawl, and infrastructure development pushes animals out of their homes. Climate change shifts seasons and water availability, affecting food sources and breeding cycles. Illegal hunting and trafficking wipe out species populations. Pollution and plastic waste destroy ecosystems. All these factors lead to decline in biodiversity and imbalance in nature. Protecting wildlife means protecting the planetâ€™s health.

Government Actions to Reduce Threats

  • Implementation of Wildlife Protection Act 1972
  • Creation of wildlife sanctuaries and national parks
  • Strict anti-poaching laws and forest patrolling
  • Project Tiger, Project Elephant and Rhino Conservation Initiatives
  • Awareness and education campaigns in rural areas
