Why Wildlife Matters?

Wildlife is the heartbeat of our planet. It helps maintain the ecological balance, supports ecosystems, and ensures the sustainability of life on Earth. Beyond environmental reasons, wildlife is crucial for scientific research, tourism, and indigenous cultures.

Ecological Balance

Wildlife regulates natural processes like pollination, seed dispersal, pest control, and nutrient cycling.

Cultural Importance

Many communities across India and the world hold spiritual and traditional values linked to animal and forest life.

Need for Wildlife Diversity

  • Maintaining food chains and ecological balance
  • Providing genetic resources and medicinal benefits
  • Preserving cultural and heritage values
  • Ensuring climate stability and preventing natural disasters

Government Acts & Initiatives

Wildlife Protection Act, 1972

Provides a legal framework to protect wild animals, birds, and plants across India. It has six schedules offering varying protection levels.

Project Tiger

Launched in 1973 to conserve Bengal tigers and their habitats. Over 50 reserves are now protected under this project.

National Biodiversity Act, 2002

Focuses on conservation and sustainable use of biodiversity, supported by Biodiversity Management Committees.

State-wise Wildlife Policies (India)

Maharashtra

Melghat Tiger Reserve and Sanjay Gandhi National Park supported by eco-tourism policies.

Assam

Kaziranga Park protected under Assam Act with strict anti-poaching law.

Rajasthan

Ranthambore and Desert Parks managed under Forest & Wildlife Department.

Kerala

Silent Valley & Periyar Parks aided by Kerala Eco Development programs.

Madhya Pradesh

'Tiger State' with active conservation in Kanha, Pench, and Bandhavgarh.

Bihar

Valmiki Reserve & Bhimbandh Sanctuary under Biodiversity Board initiative.
